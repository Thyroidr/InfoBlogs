BZOJ 2564

Rose and Scarlet are, once again, playing a game. The game is on a weighted graph $G=(V,E)$. Let the weight of vertex $v$ be $w(v)$ and the weight of edge $e$ be $c(e)$. The rule of the game is as follows:

  1. Rose and Scarlet take turns to paint vertices on the graph. Rose paints vertices red and Scarlet paints vertices pink.
  2. Painted vertices cannot be painted again.
  3. Each turn, one and only one vertex is painted.
  4. In the beginning of the game, no vertex is painted.
  5. For fairness, the number of vertices $N$ is even.
  6. After $N$ turns, Rose and Scarlet each own a set of vertices. For vertex set $S$, the score of the owner of $S$ is

$$\sum_{v \in S}w(v) + \sum_{e=(u,v) \in E \land u, v \in S}c(e)$$

  1. Rose goes first.

Assume both Rose and Scarlet play optimally, what is $S_{Rose} - S_{Scarlet}$?

Input

The first line of input includes two positive integers, $N$ and $M$, respectively representing the number of vertices and edges. $N$ is even.
The next $N$ lines each include a integer $w$, the integer in the $k$-th line represents $w(k)$.
The last $M$ lines each include three integers, $a$, $b$ and $x$, separated by space, representing a edge connecting vertex $a$ and $b$, with a weight of $x$.

Output

Output should only include one integer, Rose's score take away Scarlet's score.

Constraints

  • For 40% of inputs, $1 \le N \le 16$.
  • For 100% of inputs, $1 \le N \le 10000, 1 \le M \le 100000, -10000 \le w,c \le 10000$.

Solution

The fact that this question is placed in a post named "Greedy" is an obvious hint. Despite having the appearance of a game theory question, this question can be indeed solved with a simple greedy algorithm.

Let's consider the effect each vertex has on the final score difference ($ans$),

  • if a point is painted by Rose, its contribution of the score is $w$
  • otherwise its contribution is $-w$, since it would be painted by Scarlet
  • if neither vertices of an edge are painted by Rose, its contribution to the score is $-c$
  • if only one vertex of an edge is painted by Rose, the contribution is $0$
  • if both vertices are painted by Rose, the contribution is $c$

We can subtract all weights (both $c$'s and $w$'s) from $ans$, such that the effects become,

  • point painted by Rose: $2w$
  • point not painted by Rose: $0$
  • neither vertices of an edge: $0$
  • one vertex of an edge: $c$
  • both vertices of an edge: $2c$

Hence, the potential contribution a vertex $k$ is $2w(k)$ plus the sum of weights of all edges connected by $k$. We calculate this value, and then sort all vertices based on their potential contribution. The optimal plays for Rose and Scarlet would be to paint in the sorted order.
