DTOJ 4094

Posted on 2018-12-27 Edited on 2019-04-17 In DTOJ

Meow

Phun is a huge playground formed by $n$ pools and $n-1$ paths between pairs of them, such that one can travel from any pool to any other pool. Each paths has a length, determining the number of minutes required to travel between the two pools it connects.

To determine if they are really a match made by God, Meow has invited Neow to Phun. Meow has given $q$ tests to Neow, and each test involves Meow travelling from $u_1$ to $v_1$, and Neow travelling from $u_2$ to $v_2$. Meow will start at the $t_1$-th second, and Neow will start at the $t_2$-th second, they will both travel the shortest paths. Determine, for each test, if Meow and Neow will spend at least one second on the same edge (vertices do not count as part of edges).

Input

In the first line, $n$ and $q$, indicating the number of pools and the number of tests.

In each of the next $n-1$ lines, $u$, $v$, and $w$, representing an edge between pool $u$ and pool $v$ that requires $w$ seconds to travel.

In each of the next $q$ lines, $u_1, v_1, t_1, u_2, v_2, t_2$, representing a test.

Output

For each test, output YES if Meow and Neow will spend at least one second on the same edge, else output NO.

Sample IO

Constraints

$1 \le n, q \le 10^5$

Solution

Case bash.

We can divide the paths of Meow and Neow into each into two chains, one for when they are moving up to the LCA of $u$ and $v$, and one for moving down. They may meet in two cases, when they are both moving up or down, and when one is moving up and the other is moving down. For both cases we can determine the overlap of their chains by finding the LCA of the two deeper ends of the chains, and output NO if none of their chains overlap.

If a pair of chains do overlap, and that Meow and Neow are bothing travelling up or down on them, we need to determine if there exists an special edge on the chai. This edge needs to be strictly longer than the difference between the time Meow and Neow start travelling on the chain. If the edge does exist, than Meow and Neow will meet on the chain.

If Meow and Neow are travelling on opposing directions, we need to first ensure their time periods of travelling overlap. If so, we need to find if Meow and Neow will meet on a vertex, which can be done using a similar method to LCA. If they meet on a vertex, then they will surely not spend a second on the same edge.
